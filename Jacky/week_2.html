# Updated User Journey


During the school holiday’s, Cassidy and her family went to a tech convention where they showcased some of the newest tech start ups and products that is being release in the “near future”. While at this convention they came across a start up called Port. They Advertised themselves as a transport company using the technology of teleportation to move people from place to place using portals and checkpoints. This caught the interest of Cassidy and her family as they live a decent distance away from work and school. While looking around the Port booth, an assistant came up to them and asked if they would like to join the beta as he overheard the family talking about how useful it would be for them. With excitement and caution the family agreed to join the beta. After signing a contract as beta testers and early adopter they left and went to see other booths.

After waiting a few weeks and seeing Port all over the news and social media, the families excitement continues to grow higher and higher. The day finally came when the receive a package from Port. The package contained 4 phones for each family member. As they unboxed the phones they were greeted when opening the Port app on the phone a welcome to the Beta message and prompting them to sign in with the credentials provided by port. After entering the credentials they were prompted to create their own account as the credentials provided was to confirm their position as Beta tester/early adopters. They were prompted to enter they name, email, phone number, and a new password.

Once the account was created they were taken to the home page of the app welcoming them into Port. They were also prompted to test out the service, they were prompted to find a checkpoint near their home so they can get back, then another checkpoint somewhere else to teleport to. And so they did.

# User testing doc


What i tested:

  • The flow of the UI
  • The layout of the UI elements
  • The style of the UI
  • The General feel of the app

Who i tested on:

I have tested this UI on some of the classmates in the room and online friends

What i found out:

I have found out that i may need to updated the flow of the UI with a comment from one of the tester saying “how will you make money from this, and this looks somewhat similar to the UI of Uber”. This made me think about how i would need to add another step asking for payment of X amount. Another tester said that the recent activity and profile tabs need to be created as those menus are very important for users for usability and ease of use of the app, a settings menu should also be added. Although this is a lo-fi prototype i feel that this feed back is useful when putting time and effort into creating the Hi-fi prototype for a full user experience. Some other feedback was not helpful with further improving the UI and UX and have been left out.
